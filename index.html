About Newton Smart Gardening Club

Our Purpose

We are a student-run Friday club that meets on the 3rd floor terrace. We bring together students who enjoy growing plants and students who enjoy building things. Our purpose is clear: We grow healthy plants and we build simple systems that help plants care for themselves.

Our Story

In our first year, we focused on hydroponics. We designed and built our own system, planted herbs, and harvested them. In our second year, we moved to planting in soil and added automation with moisture, light, and temperature sensors.

What We Do

We learn by doing. Members design frames, wire sensors, write code, test parts, and observe plants. We keep things simple, safe, and reliable. We collect data from our sensors and use it to make better choices about water, light, and nutrients.

Why It Matters

Smart gardening is about solving real problems. Water is limited. Weather changes. People are busy. Automation helps. A well-designed system can water at the right time, adjust light, and protect plants. This saves time, reduces waste, and teaches useful skills.

Innovation Projects

From ideas to patents - Our research and development

Self-Adjusting Aggregation System

We are developing a self-adjusting aggregation system that measures plant height with an ultrasonic sensor and adjusts the inclined angle of the aggregation method on its own. The goal is to keep the plant at an ideal distance from light and support structures as it grows.

Research and Patent Path

We plan to move our best ideas toward a patent. To do this the team will keep clear build logs, design drawings, and test data. We will check prior art, write simple summaries of what is new, and show how our system solves a real problem in a better way.

Community Outreach

We believe in open sharing. We post regular updates on Instagram at @botanic_goat, where more than 80 people already follow our progress. We share photos, short videos, and clear notes so others can learn from our builds.

2024-2025 IoT Greenhouse Project

Applied Bioinformatics Class - 20 Week Case Study in Seoul

Executive Overview

Over twenty weeks, we designed, built, and operated an 8m² IoT-enabled urban greenhouse that demonstrates how sensors, micro-controllers, and cloud software can modernize city farming in Seoul's high-density environment (16,000+ people/km²). Using Arduino R4 WiFi, capacitive moisture sensors, and automated irrigation, we successfully grew six cooking crops while documenting every step of the journey.

Project Timeline & Progress

Weeks 1-2: Design & Procurement

Refined objectives, selected UV-resistant plastic over glass, ordered galvanized steel tubing for hoop frame, purchased cedar troughs (125×80×40cm) and 200L of soil.

Weeks 3-5: Assembly & Soil Preparation

Assembled steel frame structure, installed UV-resistant plastic sheeting, mixed custom soil blend (pH 6.5), arranged planters in optimal configuration.

Weeks 6-10: Arduino Integration

Transitioned from ESP32 to Arduino UNO R4 WiFi (Cortex-M4F, 256KB RAM), solved memory constraints, integrated HW-390 moisture sensors, established WiFi connectivity.

Weeks 11-15: Pump System & Automation

Integrated 12V SMP-01 pump with IRF-520 MOSFET, calibrated sensors (wet: 346-410, dry: 640), implemented 30-second irrigation cycles, added servo-controlled LED system.

Technical Implementation

Hardware Components

  • Arduino UNO R4 WiFi (Built-in ESP32)
  • HW-390 Capacitive Moisture Sensors (×5)
  • SMP-01 12V Water Pump
  • IRF-520 N-channel MOSFET
  • Servo Motor for LED Control
  • Full-spectrum LED Growth Lights

Software Features

  • WiFi Web Server Control Interface
  • Automatic Moisture-based Irrigation
  • 5-reading Average for Noise Reduction
  • Remote Control via Any Device
  • Threshold: 400-450 (35% moisture)
  • 60-second Monitoring Intervals

Sustainability & Innovation

Rainwater Harvesting

80-liter rain barrel with first-flush diverter removes roof debris. Float switch automatically switches to rainwater when available. During 9-day April shower period, system operated entirely on rainwater, saving 48L of tap water (>50% of weekly demand).

Social Media Impact

@botanic_goat grew from 0 to 100+ followers. Posted 10 curated updates with lo-fi/K-pop tracks. Each post combined growth timelapses, data visualizations, and engagement questions. Community actively participated with practical questions about implementation.

Engineering Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1: Voltage Mismatch

Problem: 12V pump vs 5V Arduino logic could damage components.
Solution: IRF-520 MOSFET with pull-up resistor ladder enables safe voltage bridging.

Challenge 2: Memory Constraints

Problem: ESP-01's 8KB SRAM crashed with MQTT+TLS (30% over capacity).
Solution: Upgraded to Arduino UNO R4 WiFi with 256KB RAM and built-in WiFi.

Challenge 3: Water Distribution

Problem: Leaky hose caused uneven watering - overwatered plants bolted.
Solution: 6 mini-sprinkler heads positioned 5cm above soil, tilted 15° for overlap.
